THINKING BARBERSHOP QUARTET RANKINGS

An international ranking of quartets across BHS and Alliance Organizations


The purpose behind TBQR is to recognize the talented barbershop quartets across the world, acknowledging that there can be a multitude of reasons why a group may not be able to attend BHS International.


It also recognizes that an individual performance can be impacted by factors outside a quartet's control so while the ranking places a high importance on the most recent result, it also factors in other performances.



The rankings are calcualted based on a weighted average of scores that quartets receive from competitions over a 12 month period.


  • Contests over 12 month period.

    • 12 months represents a full cycle of most contests. Averaging all scores over this period gives quartets an opportunity to have multiple performances, reducing the reliance on a single score. It also keeps the rankings relevant to current quartets, ensuring retired/inactive don't stay too long.

  • Contest scores are weighted by time.

    • With multiple contests per year, weighing a contest by it's recency ensures that the most recent performance is the most important. The time weighting is determined by dividing the score by the rank of the contest by date.

    e.g. contest ranks 1, 2, 3, 4 would be weighted 1, 0.5, 0.33, 0.25 respectively.

  • BHS International contests (Open, Varsity, Midwinter) are given maximum weighting.

    • The 3 International BHS contests are the highest ranking contests each year. They also typically have the largest judging pool, and are the feature events for top quartets. These are by default assigned the highest weighting to reflect their importance. A quartet can be top of the rankings without attending the international contests, but a high score at these contests are sustained for longer than district/regionals.

    Scores are calculated on data collected from publicly available scoresheets from BHS and Alliance organizations.
